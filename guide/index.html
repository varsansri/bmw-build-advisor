The Models You Should Know

BMW 3 Series (G20) — The Sweet Spot
From $43,000 (new) / $22,000 (used)
Best balance of driver engagement, tech, practicality, and running costs. The 330i (B48 engine) is BMW's most proven modern engine. Rear-wheel drive. Genuinely fun.
Best for: First BMW buyers, daily commuters who want to enjoy driving
BMW M2 (G87) — Pure Sports Car
From $65,000 (new) / $55,000 (used)
S58 twin-turbo inline-6, 453hp, rear-wheel drive, no AWD option. The most pure, analog M car of the current generation. Manual gearbox available.
Best for: Enthusiasts, track-day drivers, those who prioritize driving over everything
BMW X3 (G01/G45) — Family First
From $47,000 (new) / $25,000 (used)
Best-in-class dynamics for a compact SUV. xDrive AWD, excellent interior, 480L boot space. The go-to family BMW that doesn't compromise driver enjoyment.
Best for: Families with kids, mixed urban/country driving, school runs + weekend trips
BMW i4 — Electric Done Right
From $57,000 (new)
The best electric BMW. Gran Coupé body, 300–537hp, M50 version = 537hp. Feels like a proper BMW to drive. 300+ mile range on the eDrive40 variant.
Best for: Those wanting an EV that doesn't compromise the BMW driving feel
