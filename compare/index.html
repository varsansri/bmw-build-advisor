Who Should Buy Each?

Buy the M2 if...
You want the purest, most focused M experience. Smaller, lighter, rear-wheel drive only, lower price. The last "small and simple" M car. If you love driving above all else, the M2 is the answer. Track days, twisty roads, a car that talks to you.
Buy the M3 if...
You need practicality without compromise. 4-door body fits kids, passengers, and luggage without making daily life difficult. The Touring (wagon) version adds 500L boot space. Still a serious performance car, just more usable.
Buy the M4 if...
You want M3 performance in a coupe body. Looks sharper, slightly more focused than the M3. The xDrive Competition is the fastest of the three in a straight line. The Cabriolet is for those who want open-air M performance.
Honest verdict: The M2 is the enthusiast's choice. The M3 Competition xDrive is the fastest daily driver. The M4 Competition xDrive is what you buy when you want to turn heads. All three use the same excellent S58 engine. None of them will disappoint you.
